Gurez Valley Travel Guide: Kashmir's Best Kept Secret

Often called the "Switzerland of India" in the truest sense, Gurez Valley is a landscape frozen in time. Located near the Line of Control (LoC), this valley was closed to tourists for years. Now open, it offers an adventure unlike any other in Kashmir.

About Gurez Valley

Gurez is located about 123km from Srinagar in the Bandipora district. Situated at 8,000 ft, it is surrounded by snow-capped peaks. The Kishanganga River flows through the valley, adding to its surreal beauty. The locals are "Dards" (Shins) who have a distinct culture and language.

How to Reach (The Journey)

Reaching Gurez is an adventure in itself.

  • Route: Srinagar -> Bandipora -> Razdan Pass -> Gurez (Dawar).
  • Razdan Pass: At 11,672 ft, this is one of the most dangerous yet scenic passes in Kashmir. The views are jaw-dropping.
  • Time: It takes about 6-7 hours from Srinagar.

Key Attractions

  • Habba Khatoon Peak: The iconic pyramid-shaped mountain named after the poetess Habba Khatoon. It dominates the skyline of Dawar.
  • Kishanganga River: Crystal clear waters perfect for trout fishing and riverside camping.
  • Tulail Valley: Drive further ahead (40km) to see traditional log-house villages like Sheikhpora.

Where to Stay?

Tourism infrastructure is developing.

  • JKTDC Dak Bunglow: Government-run, needs booking.
  • Hotels/Guest Houses: Kaka Palace and Wood Vibes are popular options in Dawar.
  • Camping: Many dedicated camping sites have come up near the river.

Permits & Necessary Info

Since Gurez is a border area:

  • ID Proof: Compulsory at Razdan Pass check post. Aadhaar Card / Voter ID is essential.
  • Online Permit: Usually not required for Indian citizens now, only physical check at the post. Foreigners may need special permission.
  • Best Time: June to September. The road is closed in winter due to heavy snow.
