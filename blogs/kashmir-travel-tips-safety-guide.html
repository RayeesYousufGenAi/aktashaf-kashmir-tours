Is Kashmir Safe? Essential Travel Tips & Safety Guide 2026

The most common question we get at AKTASHAF KASHMIR is: "Is it safe to visit Kashmir?" The short answer is YES. Kashmir sees millions of tourists annually who return with only happy memories. However, there are some important things you need to know.

Safety & Tips Checklist
  • 1. Is it Really Safe?
  • 2. Vital Mobile Network Rule
  • 3. Avoiding Tourist Scams
  • 4. What to Pack?
  • 5. Cultural Etiquette

1. Is it Really Safe?

Yes. Tourism is the backbone of Kashmir's economy, and locals are extremely warm and protective of tourists. You can safely visit Srinagar, Gulmarg, Pahalgam, and Sonamarg. Just follow standard travel advice: avoid venturing into unknown areas late at night.

2. Vital Mobile Network Rule (IMPORTANT!)

Prepaid SIM cards from outside J&K DO NOT WORK in Kashmir.

  • Solution: Carry a POSTPAID SIM card (Jio or Airtel work best).
  • Alternative: You can buy a temporary tourist SIM upon arrival, but activation takes 24 hours.
  • Note: BSNL Postpaid works well in remote areas.

3. Avoiding Tourist Scams

Like any tourist spot, there are minor scams to watch out for:

  • Pony Rides: Always agree on the price BEFORE getting on the horse. Rates are fixed by the government, ask to see the rate card.
  • Shopping: Don't buy "Saffron" from roadside vendors. Buy only from certified shops to avoid fake products.
  • guides: Hire only registered guides.

4. What to Pack?

  • Winter (Nov-Mar): Heavy thermals, down jackets, gloves, woollen caps.
  • Summer (Apr-Oct): Light cottons for day, light jacket for evening.
  • Essentials: Sunscreen (high altitude sun is strong), sunglasses, moisturizer, power bank.

5. Cultural Etiquette

Kashmir has a conservative culture.

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious shrines (cover your head).
  • Remove shoes before entering houseboats or religious places.
  • Ask for permission before clicking photos of locals.
