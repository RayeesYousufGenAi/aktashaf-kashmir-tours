Shopping in Kashmir Guide 2026: What to Buy & How to Avoid Scams

Taking a piece of Kashmir back home is mandatory! But with so many options and qualities, shopping here can be overwhelming. From the world's finest Saffron to the softest Pashmina, here is your definitive guide to buying authentic Kashmiri products.

Shopping Checklist
  • Pashmina Shawls
  • Kashmiri Saffron
  • Dry Fruits
  • Carpets & Paper Mache
  • Where to Shop?

1. Pashmina Shawls

Real Pashmina is made from the wool of the Changthangi goat found in Ladakh. It is incredibly light yet warm.

  • The Ring Test: A pure Pashmina shawl is so fine it can pass through a wedding ring (though this is not the only test).
  • Price: A real hand-spun Pashmina starts from ₹10,000 and can go up to lakhs. Check for the GI (Geographical Indication) tag.

2. Kashmiri Saffron (Kesar)

Pampore in Kashmir is one of the few places in the world where saffron grows. It is the most expensive spice.

  • Identification: Real saffron strands are dark red with a trumpet-shaped tip. If it's instantly turning water red, it might be artificial. Real saffron releases color slowly (golden yellow).

3. Dry Fruits (Walnuts & Almonds)

Kashmir produces organic walnuts and almonds.

  • Kagzi Walnuts: These have a soft shell that can be broken with one hand.
  • Mamra Almonds: Rich in oil and oddly shaped, but far superior in taste to California almonds.

4. Carpets & Paper Mache

  • Hand-Knotted Silk Carpets: A lifetime investment. The quality depends on the number of knots per square inch.
  • Paper Mache: Beautifully painted boxes, vases, and Christmas ornaments made from paper pulp. Great for budget souvenirs.

Where to Shop in Srinagar?

  • Lal Chowk: The main commercial hub for general shopping and dry fruits.
  • Polo View Market: Pedestrian-only market famous for handicrafts and Paper Mache.
  • Boulevard Road: Line of shops facing Dal Lake (good for browsing, but bargain hard!).
