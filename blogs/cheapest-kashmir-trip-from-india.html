How to Plan the Cheapest Kashmir Trip from India (2026 Guide)

Is Kashmir only for the rich? Absolutely not! Thousands of students, backpackers, and budget travelers visit Kashmir every year. With smart planning, you can explore the valley on a shoestring budget. Here is AKTASHAF KASHMIR's guide to saving money without compromising on fun.

Money Saving Hacks
  • 1. The Train Hack (Cheap Transport)
  • 2. Travel in Off-Season
  • 3. Budget Accommodation Tips
  • 4. Eat Where Locals Eat
  • 5. Use Shared Cabs

1. The Train Hack: Reach Jammu by Train

Flights to Srinagar can be expensive (₹8,000+ one way). The cheapest way is:

  1. Take a train to Jammu Tawi Railway Station (₹500 - ₹2000).
  2. From Jammu, take a shared cab or bus to Srinagar (₹800 - ₹1200).
  3. Tip: The train link to Srinagar is almost complete, but for now, the road trip is necessary.

2. Travel in Off-Season

Avoid May/June and December/January if you are on a tight budget.

  • Best Cheap Months: Late February, March (before Tulips), August, November.
  • Benefit: Hotels offer up to 50% discount during these times.

3. Choosing Budget Stays

You don't need a 5-star hotel to enjoy Kashmir.

  • Guest Houses: Look for homestays in Rajbagh or Dalgate area. They are clean and cheap (₹1500/night).
  • Hostels: Srinagar now has backpacker hostels starting at ₹600/bed.
  • Houseboats: Choose a houseboat on Nigeen Lake or backwaters for cheaper rates than main Dal Lake.

4. Eat Where Locals Eat

Tourist restaurants charge premium prices. Instead:

  • Eat at "Krishna Dhaba" for pure veg food at great prices.
  • Try street food like Nadru Monje (Lotus stem fritters) and Tujji (BBQ skewers).
  • Drink "Noon Chai" (Salt tea) with local bread for a cheap, filling breakfast.

5. Use Shared Cabs

Private taxis are the biggest expense. Use the local sumo service:

  • Srinagar to Gulmarg: Catch a shared cab from Tangmarg stand.
  • Srinagar to Pahalgam: Shared cabs run from TRC (Tourist Reception Centre).
